        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        Employees or agents of Swift are alleged to have paid GBP 180,000 in bribes to tax officials in two Nigerian states between 2008 and 2009 in order to avoid, reduce or delay paying taxes on behalf of workers placed by Swift.  The officials are identified as agents of the Rivers State Board of Internal Revenue, and the Lagos State Board of Internal Revenue.

        <h2>HOW CONDUCT WAS DISCOVERED</h2>
        It is not yet known how the conduct was discovered.  The case was accepted for investigation by the SFO in August 2010 following a referral by City of London Police.
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT RESULT</h2>
        United Kingdom<br /><br />On 17 December 2012, four individuals were charged by the Serious Fraud Office with conspiracy to corrupt.  The four are Paul Jacobs, Bharat Sodha, Nidhi Vyas, and Trevor Bruce. A preliminary hearing is scheduled for 22 February 2013.<br /><br />The Swift Group is cooperating with the SFO in its investigation.  The Company is not facing criminal charges.
