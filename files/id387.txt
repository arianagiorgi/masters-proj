        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        Following an investigation by the Indian Central Bureau of Investigation in 2009, the Indian authorities concluded that it had sufficient evidence that IMI was involved in bribing Sudipta Ghosh, former director of Ordnance Factories, as well as other Indian officers.  The company had won a contract to set up the Ordnance Factory Board's 40th factory, in Nalanda in the state of Bihar.  <br /><br />

        <h2>HOW CONDUCT WAS DISCOVERED</h2>
        
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT RESULT</h2>
        On 6 March 2012, India debarred IMI from bidding on any defense contracts for ten years.  IMI intends to appeal the decision.<br /><br />Then on 17 April 2012, the Indian Ministry of Defense fined IMI for breach of an integrity agreement in an arms contract.  The Ministry seized a USD 70 million (INR 2,240,000) bank guarantee of IMI.  An "integrity pact" is an undertaking by foreign companies that they will not enter into corrupt activity. <br /><br />On 17 September 2014, India announced that it was lifting the ban against IMI, and will allow the company to bid on its tenders.<br /><br />
