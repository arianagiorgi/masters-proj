        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        On May 27, 2010, the U.S. Department of Justice ("DOJ") requested a stay in a federal civil suit in Houston between Aluminium Bahrain BSC ("Alba") and Japan's Sojitz Group and its U.S. subsidiary.  Alba, Bahrain's majority state-owned aluminum producer, filed a USD $31 million suit against Sojitz for allegedly paying USD 15 million in bribes to Alba managers between 1993 to 2006 in order to secure below-market discounts on aluminum.<br /><br />The investigation into Sojitz was related to a separate ongoing investigation into U.S. aluminum producer Alcoa, where the DOJ intervened in a similar civil suit brought by Alba against Alcoa over alleged bribery (see Compendium summary for "Alcoa").  <br /><br />According to Sojitz officials, the DOJ advised the company in April 2012 that their investigation had been discontinued.<br />

        <h2>HOW CONDUCT WAS DISCOVERED</h2>
        It appears that the DOJ investigation commenced following Alba's initiation of a federal civil suit against Sojitz for alleged bribery of its employees.
