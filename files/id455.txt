        <h2>DESCRIPTION OF BUSINESS</h2>
        Manufacture, sale and distribution of electrical wire and communications cables
        
        <h2>CORPORATE HEADQUARTERS</h2>
        Osaka, Japan
        
        <h2>NATIONALITY OF FOREIGN OFFICIALS</h2>
        Indonesia<br />
        
        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        According to the Jakarta Globe's 4 December 2012 report, Shiokawa Toshio, president of Onamba Indonesia, was found guilty of paying a bribe to a judge on the Bandung Industrial Relations Court through Onamba Indonesia's human resources manager Odi Juanda. According to the article, the bribe amount was IDR 200 million (USD 20,800). The article also says that the bribe was paid shortly after the labor court judge ruled in favor of Onamba Indonesia in a labor dispute, permitting the company to fire workers who had joined a strike.

        <h2>HOW CONDUCT WAS DISCOVERED</h2>
        Unspecified
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT RESULT</h2>
        The Jakarta Globe reported on 4 December 2012 that Shiokawa Toshio was found guilty of bribing Imas Dianasari and sentenced to three years in prison. Toshio was also required to pay a fine of IDR 200 million (approximately USD 20,800) or spend six additional months in prison. According to the Jakarta Globe story, Toshio (who is a Japanese national) is "Indonesia's first foreign graft convict." The Jakarta Globe also reported that Imas Dianasari (the judge who accepted the bribe) and Odi Juanda (the human resources manager who paid the bribe) were sentenced to six years and four years in prison, respectively.
        
        <h2>KEY TAKEAWAYS</h2>
        <ul><li>The fact that a defendant paid a bribe through another person will not relieve him of criminal liability.</li></ul>
