        In Fresenius' Form 20-F filed on 1 August 2012 with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, the company disclosed that it had received "communications alleging certain conduct that may violate the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act ('FCPA') and other anti-bribery laws."<br /><br />The Company noted further that its Audit and Corporate Governance Committee of the Company's Supervisory Board was conducting an internal review, and that it had engaged counsel for this purpose, and voluntarily notified the SEC and DOJ.  On 6 May 2014, Fresenius announced in its Form 6-K filed with the SEC that it had initiated an internal investigation following allegations that its conduct in certain countries outside the U.S. and Germany might violate anti-bribery laws.
