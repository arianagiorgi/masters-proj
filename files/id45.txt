        <h1>PACIFIC CONSULTANTS INTERNATIONAL</h1>
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT AGENCY</h2>
        Japan: Tokyo District Public Prosecutors Office <br />
        
        <h2>ENTITIES/INDIVIDUALS INVOLVED</h2>
        Pacific Consultants International Co., Ltd. ("PCI")<br />Masayoshi Taga (former President of PCI)<br />Kunio Takasu (former Executive Director of PCI)<br />Haruo Sakashita (former Director of PCI)<br />Tsuneo Sakano (former Chief of Hanoi office of PCI)<br />
        
        <h2>DESCRIPTION OF BUSINESS</h2>
        Construction consulting
        
        <h2>CORPORATE HEADQUARTERS</h2>
        Tokyo, Japan
        
        <h2>NATIONALITY OF FOREIGN OFFICIALS</h2>
        Vietnam<br />
        
        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        According to news agency reports, in December 2003 Takasu, Sakashita and Sakano made an improper payment of approximately US$600,000 to a senior official of the East-West Highway and Water Environment Project Management Unit of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam in Ho Chi Minh, in return for awarding consulting contracts for the Saigon East-West Highway Construction Project ("the Project").<br /><br />Press reports also indicate that in August 2006 Taga, Takasu and Sakashita made an improper payment of approximately US$220,000 to the same senior official in return for awarding consulting contracts for the Project.
        
        <h2>RATIO OF IMPROPER PAYMENTS TO BUSINESS ADVANTAGE</h2>

</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
        
        <h2>HOW CONDUCT WAS DISCOVERED</h2>
        Unspecified
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT RESULT</h2>
        News agencies reported that on August 25, 2008, the TDPPO charged PCI, Taga, Sakashita, Takasu and Sakano with violation of the Unfair Competition Prevention Law of Japan ("UCPLJ").<br /><br />On January 29, 2009, the Tokyo District Court reportedly found:<br /><br />(1) PCI guilty and fined it JPY 70,000,000 (approx. US$780,500);<br />(2) Sakashita guilty and sentenced him to two years in prison with a three-year suspended sentence;<br />(3) Takasu guilty and sentenced him to one year and eight months in prison with a three-year suspended sentence; and<br />(4) Sakano guilty and sentenced him to one year and six months in prison with a three-year suspended sentence.<br /><br />On March 24, 2009, the Tokyo District Court reportedly found Taga guilty and sentenced him two years and six months in prison with a three-year suspended sentence for violation of the UCPLJ and an unrelated fraud case.<br /><br />On October 18, 2010, the Vietnamese official at issue was sentenced to life in prison for accepting the bribe.
