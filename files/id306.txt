        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        In July 2010, it was reported that Deutsche Bahn was conducting an internal investigation into bribery allegations involving its subsidiary, DB International, in connection with rail-related contracts in Algeria, Rwanda and Greece.  The company has reportedly begun terminating employees and consultants in connection with the matter.  Deutsche reportedly disclosed its investigation to state prosecutors in 2009.<br /><br />On 18 June 2013, Deutsche Bahn shut down its office in Athens due to the bribery scandal, German newspaper Sueddeutsche Zeitung reported. Citing investigation findings from the prosecutorâ€™s office in Frankfurt, the Deutsche Bahn subsidiary in Greece allegedly diverted EU 315,000 to bribe contract givers. The diverted money was given to secure contracts at the Athens Metro.  Deutsche Bahn dismissed more than 30 employees of Deutsche Bahn International: directors, project managers, etc. Numerous contracts with dubious consultants were terminated. Deutsche Bahn imposed a requirement that its foreign offices may only have a maximum of 500 euros in cash converted into the local currency and any cash payments over EUR 100 are prohibited.
