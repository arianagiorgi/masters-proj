        <h1>CATLER UNISERVICE</h1>
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT AGENCY</h2>
        Argentina: Federal Criminal Court, City of Buenos Aires<br />
        
        <h2>ENTITIES/INDIVIDUALS INVOLVED</h2>
        Catler Uniservice (an Argentine-Bolivian consortium) <br />Sica Metalurgica (Argentine supplier to Catler Uniservice)<br />Lito Gonella e Hijos de Santa Fe (Argentine supplier to Catler Uniservice)<br />
        
        <h2>DESCRIPTION OF BUSINESS</h2>
        Oil exploration and hydroelectric plants
        
        <h2>CORPORATE HEADQUARTERS</h2>
        Buenos Aires, Argentina
        
        <h2>NATIONALITY OF FOREIGN OFFICIALS</h2>
        Bolivia<br />
        
        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        Catler Uniservice, an Argentine and Bolivian joint venture, allegedly paid bribes to Bolivian government officials at the state-owned energy company, Yacimientos Petroliferos Fiscales Bolivianos ("YPFB"), in order to obtain a USD 86 million contract to build a gas processing plant in Santa Cruz, Bolivia, in 2008.  Sica Metalurgica  and Lito Gonella e Hijos de Santa Fe were Argentine suppliers to Catler Uniservice. <br /><br />In January 2012, Bolivian state oil company Yacimientos Petroliferos Fiscales Bolivianos’s former chief executive officer Santos Ramirez was sentenced to 12 years in prison on corruption charges, La Razon reported. Ramirez was convicted of accepting a $450,000 bribe from Argentine firm Catler Uniservice in exchange for awarding an $86 million contract to build a liquefied petroleum gas plant in 2009, the La Paz-based newspaper said. Catler’s general manager Jorge O’Connor was shot dead in La Paz while bringing the money to Ramirez, according to La Razon.

        Catler Uniservices Chief Executive Officer, Jorge OConnor, was assaulted and murdered in January 2009.  An envelope with USD 450,000 in cash was stolen.  The investigation of the murder triggered the investigation for bribery.

        The investigation is ongoing.  The Investigating Magistrate (Judge d'Instruction) reportedly is waiting for an assistance requested to Bolivia.
