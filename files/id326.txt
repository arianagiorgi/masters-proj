        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        On 13 January 2011, Nigeria's Economic and Financial Crimes Commission (EFCC) detained twelve oil-industry employees, including several Murphy Shipping employees.  Nigeria’s anti-corruption agency said in a statement it arrested 12 executives from multinational oil-servicing firms over an alleged $100 million bribery scheme, the Wall Street Journal reported. The individuals were later released.<br /><br />Those arrested included executives from Noble Inc., Tidewater Inc., Murphy Shipping and Transocean Ltd., the EFCC said in a statement. The arrests are connected to the EFCC's domestic bribery investigation deriving from the foreign bribery settlements reached by Panalpina and several oilfield services companies with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (“SEC”) and U.S. Department of Justice (“DOJ”) in November 2010.
