        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        According to press reports, Actavis, an Icelandic generic pharmaceutical company, was fined CZK 750,000 (EUR 30,000) in August 2009 by the Czech State Institute for Drug Control (SUKL) for having covered the costs of a holiday on the Egyptian coast for a group of Czech doctors in exchange for the prescription of its medicines.  <br /><br />It appears that technically Actavis was penalized for violating the law on advertising, rather than for bribery.
