        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        In order to obtain contracts to build liquefied natural gas facilities on Bonny Island, Nigeria, beginning in 1995, the TSKJ joint venture composed of Technip S.A., Snamprogetti Netherlands B.V., Kellogg Brown & Root Inc. (KBR), and JGC Corporation hired an individual named Jeffrey Tesler, and the Marubeni Corporation, to pay bribes to Nigerian government officials. Over a period of nine years, TSKJ paid approximately $132 million to a corporation controlled by Tesler, and $51 million to Marubeni Corporation, with the understanding the part of these sums would be used to pay Nigerian officials for facilitating and awarding four construction contracts, worth over $6 billion.<br /><br />For a detailed account of the role played by each of the joint venture partners in the bribery scheme, see the individual Compendium entries for those entities.
