        <h1>AMERICAN TOTALISATOR</h1>
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT AGENCY</h2>
        United States: Department of Justice<br />
        
        <h2>ENTITIES/INDIVIDUALS INVOLVED</h2>
        American Totalisator Company, Inc. (wholly-owned subsidiary of Chicago-based General Instruments Corporation) ("American Totalisator")
        
        <h2>DESCRIPTION OF BUSINESS</h2>
        Manufacture and sale of totalisator systems
        
        <h2>CORPORATE HEADQUARTERS</h2>
        Hunt Valley, Maryland, United States
        
        <h2>NATIONALITY OF FOREIGN OFFICIALS</h2>
        Greece<br />
        
        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        The U.S. Department of Justice (“DOJ”) alleged that American Totalisator violated the FCPA's anti-bribery provisions in connection with improper payments made to officials at the Horse Races Administration of Greece between 1985 and 1993 in order to secure the sale of a totlisator system and spare parts for the Phaleron racetrack in Athens. [A totalisator is a device used to display betting information, particularly at horse racing tracks.]  The payments were made through the company's Greek agent, O. Nicholas Katsanis.<br /><br />

        <h2>HOW CONDUCT WAS DISCOVERED</h2>
        Unspecified
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT RESULT</h2>
        On 21 January 1993, without admitting or denying the DOJ's allegations, American Totalisator agreed to the entry of a permanent injunction enjoining them from future violations of the FCPA.  
        
        <h2>KEY TAKEAWAYS</h2>
        <ul><li>The FCPA prohibits both direct payments and indirect payments (through an agent or other intermediary) to foreign officials for the purpose of securing an improper advantage.</li></ul>
