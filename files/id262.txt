        <h2>ENFORCEMENT AGENCY</h2>
        United States: Department of Justice<br />
        
        <h2>ENTITIES/INDIVIDUALS INVOLVED</h2>
        Samsung Rental Limited ("SSRT")<br />Gi-Hwan Jeong (owner of SSRT)
        
        <h2>DESCRIPTION OF BUSINESS</h2>
        Telecommunications services
        
        <h2>CORPORATE HEADQUARTERS</h2>
        South Korea
        
        <h2>NATIONALITY OF FOREIGN OFFICIALS</h2>
        United States<br />
        
        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        In November 2008, the DOJ charged Gi-Hwan Jeong, owner of SSRT, with one count of conspiracy to defraud the United States, to commit wire fraud and to commit bribery, and one count of bribery.   Between 2001 and 2006, Jeong paid over USD 70,000 in bribes (in the form of cash, entertainment, travel, gifts, and other things of value) to employees of the Army Air Force Exchange Service ("AAFES") in order to secure a USD 206 million contract for SSRT to provide telecommunication services to AAFES customers.

        <h2>ENFORCEMENT RESULT</h2>
        On June 11, 2009, Gi-Hwan Jeong pleaded guilty in U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Texas.  On November 10, 2009, he was sentenced to five years in prison and a ordered to pay a USD 50,000 fine.<br /><br />Jeong's co-conspirators in the AAFES were prosecuted as well.
