        <h2>DESCRIPTION OF BUSINESS</h2>
        Provider of IT services
        
        <h2>CORPORATE HEADQUARTERS</h2>
        India
        
        <h2>NATIONALITY OF FOREIGN OFFICIALS</h2>
        Chile<br />
        
        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        In January 2009, Chilean prosecutors brought charges of bribery and fraud against four employees working for the Chilean subsidiary of a multinational IT company headquartered in India, as well as the head of the Chilean Civil Registry Office, in connection with a USD 80 million IT contract issued by the Civil Registry Office to the company.  An advisor to the Civil Registry Office allegedly provided confidential information to the company regarding the tender.  The contract was later annulled because of the corruption allegations.<br /><br />An investigation is also underway into whether employees of the company paid bribes to a Chilean police officer who destroyed evidence related to the Civil Registry Office case.<br /><br />No allegations or charges have been made against the company itself.

        <h2>HOW CONDUCT WAS DISCOVERED</h2>
        Chilean prosecutor Alejandra Godoy launched the investigation following the identification of irregularities in the tender process.   The scandal prompted President Michelle Bachelet to demand the removal of certain staff at the Civil Registry Office, while the Comptroller's Office ordered the cancellation of the contract.<br />
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT RESULT</h2>
