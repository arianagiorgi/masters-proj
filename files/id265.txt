        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        According to press reports, in 2009 Namibian authorities began investigating whether Nuctech, a Chinese company that makes security scanning equipment for airports, customs warehouses and other traffic points, had paid bribes to Namibian officials in order to win business in 2008.  Nuctech's Africa representative, Yang Fan, and two Namibian officials have been accused of bribery in connection with a USD 55.3 million contract to install Nuctech scanners at customs inspection points in Namibia.<br /><br />Nuctech was, until recently, headed by Hu Haifeng, the son of China's president Hu Jintao.  Nuctech is owned by China's Tsinghua University.
