        <h1>SILICON CONTRACTORS</h1>
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT AGENCY</h2>
        United States: Department of Justice<br />
        
        <h2>ENTITIES/INDIVIDUALS INVOLVED</h2>
        Silicon Contractors, Inc. (formerly known as Tech-Sil, Inc.) ("Silicon")<br />Herbert D. Hughes (officer of Silicon)<br />Ronald R. Richardson (officer of Silicon)<br />Richard L. Noble (officer of Silicon)<br />John Sherman (officer of Silicon)
        
        <h2>DESCRIPTION OF BUSINESS</h2>
        Manufacture, sale and installation of radiation and fire-stop penetration seals for use in nuclear power plants
        
        <h2>CORPORATE HEADQUARTERS</h2>
        Texas, United States
        
        <h2>NATIONALITY OF FOREIGN OFFICIALS</h2>
        Mexico<br />
        
        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        On June 27, 1985, Silicon was charged with violating the anti-bribery provisions of the FCPA in connection with a $132,000 improper payment made by a Silicon officer to one or more officials at the Comision Federal de Electricidad ("CFE"), Mexico's state-owned electric monopoly on June 27, 1980.  The payment was made to influence the award of a contract to manufacture and install radiation and fire-stop penetration seals for a nuclear power plant in Laguna Verde, Mexico.<br /><br />The DOJ brought a civil injunctive action against Hughes, Richardson, Noble and Sherman, officers of Silicon, for their roles in the scheme.

        <h2>HOW CONDUCT WAS DISCOVERED</h2>
        Unspecified
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT RESULT</h2>
        The corporation pleaded guilty to one count of violating the FCPA's anti-bribery provisions, agreed to a permanent civil injunction, and was fined $150,000.<br /><br />In a related civil case, Hughes, Richardson, Noble and Sherman agreed to the entry of permanent injunctions prohibiting them from future violations of the FCPA.<br />
        
        <h2>KEY TAKEAWAYS</h2>
        <ul><li>The DOJ can bring either criminal or civil charges for violations of the FCPA.</li></ul>
