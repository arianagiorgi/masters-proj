        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        A Chinese businessman, Miao Keyin, was arrested and charged by the Ethiopian Revenues and Customs Authority ("ERCA") for bribing an Ethiopian customs official to avoid paying the appropriate duty on six containers of cargo being imported for a blanket factory.

        Unspecified
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT RESULT</h2>
        According to press reports, on 27 March 2009, the 10th Criminal Bench at the Federal First Instance Court in Ethiopia found Miao Keyin guilty on two counts of importing undeclared goods and of bribing a customs officer.  He was sentenced to a 15-year prison term.  The company he worked for was unnamed.  <br /><br />The court also found Mesfin Zebene, an Ethiopian customs official, guilty of attempt to receive bribes and for failure to report to superiors on the inconsistency of the goods brought in and what was declared to customs on the commercial invoices.  He was sentenced to a 10-year prison term.
