        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        According to press reports, in 2009 the Anti-Corruption Unit of the Slovak Police Corps commenced an investigation into the alleged bribery of Turks and Caicos officials - including former premier Michael Misick - by Slovakian investment group Istrokapital and its boss, Mario Hoffmann.  Hoffmann allegedly paid bribes and gave various improper benefits to Misick in order to secure government approval and preferential treatment in connection with a large resort development on the island.<br /><br />Turks & Caicos is a British Overseas Territory.   The UK Home Office is investigating Misick and other officials involved in the matter.  In August 2009, the UK suspended Turks and Caicos' self-government due to allegations of systemic corruption.
