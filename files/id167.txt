        <h2>ENTITIES/INDIVIDUALS INVOLVED</h2>
        Blohm & Voss AG (now a subsidiary of ThyssenKrupp Marine Systems)<br />Howaldtswerke Deutsche Werft AG (now a subsidiary of ThyssenKrupp Marine Systems)<br />Thyssen Rheinstahl Technik (subsidiary of ThyssenKrupp)
        
        <h2>DESCRIPTION OF BUSINESS</h2>
        Manufacture and sale of naval ships
        
        <h2>CORPORATE HEADQUARTERS</h2>
        Düsseldorf, Germany
        
        <h2>NATIONALITY OF FOREIGN OFFICIALS</h2>
        Angola<br />South Africa<br />
        
        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        The German Frigate Consortium (the "Consortium") has not acknowledged in any public filings the existence of a 2006-2008 investigation by the Düsseldorf Public Prosecutor's Office into allegations that members of the Consortium made improper payments to South African officials in connection with a late 1990s tender to provide corvettes or improper payments to Angolan officials in connection with a 2006 tender.   The investigation was reported by the Der Spiegel newspaper in February 2007.

        <h2>HOW CONDUCT WAS DISCOVERED</h2>
        The investigation was reported by Der Spiegel in February 2007. 
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT RESULT</h2>
        A ThyssenKrupp Marine Systems spokesperson was cited by South Africa's Mail & Guardian newspaper in June 2008 as stating that the investigation was closed without charges or penalty.  However, it appears that at least other aspects of the German investigation may be ongoing.
