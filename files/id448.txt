        <h2>CORPORATE HEADQUARTERS</h2>

        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        Ivo Sanader was accused of taking a bribe of HRK (Croatian kuna) 3.6 million (USD 610,000) between 1994 and 1995 during his term as deputy foreign minister.  The bribe was received in exchange for the bank's issuing a loan of ATS (Austrian schillings) 140 million (USD 14 million) to the government of Croatia.<br /> <br />

        <h2>ENFORCEMENT RESULT</h2>
        Sanader led the Croatian Democratic Union, and was elected prime minister in 2003 and again in 2007. He left office without explanation in 2009.  He was arrested in Austria in December 2010, and extradited to Croatia in July 2011.<br /><br />After a trial in the district court, on 20 November 2012, Ivo Sanader  was sentenced to ten years in prison, having been found guilty of bribery and abuse of power involving both Hypo Alpe-Adria and Mol Nyrt, a Hungarian oil refining company.
