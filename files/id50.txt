        <h1>IMPSA</h1>
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT AGENCY</h2>
        Argentina: Federal Criminal Court, City of Buenos Aires<br />
        
        <h2>ENTITIES/INDIVIDUALS INVOLVED</h2>
        Industrias Metalurgicas Pescarmona SA ("IMPSA")<br />Board members and staff of IMPSA<br />
        
        <h2>DESCRIPTION OF BUSINESS</h2>
        Hydroelectric power plants  
        
        <h2>CORPORATE HEADQUARTERS</h2>
        Buenos Aires, Argentina
        
        <h2>NATIONALITY OF FOREIGN OFFICIALS</h2>
        Philippines<br />
        
        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        Board members of IMPSA are under criminal investigation for allegedly bribing several Philippine public officials, including Former Secretary of Justice Arnoldo Perez, to obtain a contract to repair the Caliraya-Botocan-Kalayaan ("CBK") Power Complex in Laguna at a cost of USD 470 million.

</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
        
        <h2>HOW CONDUCT WAS DISCOVERED</h2>
        The bribe was reported by former Philippine President Estrada from jail, a couple of weeks after President Arroyo took office in 2001.  He was jailed for corruption from was reported by the Philippine press as early as November 2002, when the Swiss authorities request cooperation from the Philippines to identify the origin of funds reported as suspicious by a financial institution. <br /><br />In 2003, the Argentine press reported the case but the investigation was initiated only in 2006 after the OECD Group on Bribery requested the Argentine delegation to take action. 
        
        <h2>ENFORCEMENT RESULT</h2>
        The investigation is ongoing.  The Investigating Magistrate (Judge d'Instruction), Judge Sergio Torres, has requested assistance from Switzerland and the Philippines.
        
        <h2>KEY TAKEAWAYS</h2>
        <ul><li>To be determined</li></ul>
