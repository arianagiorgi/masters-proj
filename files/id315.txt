        <h2>SUMMARY OF ALLEGATIONS</h2>
        According to press reports on 15 December 2010, two Chinese businessmen - Tao Zili and Liu Xiaoting of Guangdong-based Foshan Meijiao Trading Co. - were arrested by Mexican authorities for allegedly attempting to bribe officials at the state-owned petroleum company, Petr√≥leos Mexicanos (Pemex), in order to secure contracts to buy polyethylene at preferential prices.  The two businessmen allegedly handed USD 3,000, hidden among documents, to two officials at Pemex's overseas commercial branch, PMI Comercio Internacional, and offered to pay the officials USD 25,000 to USD 60,000 a month for the preferential pricing.

        According to press reports, the Pemex officials reported the bribe offers to prosecutors, after which the businessmen were arrested.  The defendants reportedly described the attempted payments as "gifts for the holidays."
